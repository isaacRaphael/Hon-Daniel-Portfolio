About

Daniel Azibanyenami Iworiso-Markson is a businessman, media consultant, publisher, philanthropist, author and politician. He is the founder of the Robert Sunday Iworiso Foundation, a Niger Delta based Non-governmental organization committed to transforming lives. He is the publisher of FirstNews, an online and print newspaper with strong bias for investigative reporting.

A DANIEL FOR THESE TIMES

PROFILE OF DANIEL IWORISO-MARKSON

  • Birthday: June 14, 1970
  • Age: 30
  • If ever time,  experience and character have prepared a man for higher service the confluence of all three is the story of Daniel Iworiso-Markson.

His life story began when he was born on June 14, 1970, in Opume, Ogbia Local Government Area of Bayelsa State, Nigeria. His parents, Mr. Robert Sunday Iworiso-Markson and Mrs. Salome Oginasisi Iworiso-Markson (both deceased) left Opume for Lagos in search of greener pastures in the early 1950s but were forced to return to the village when the Nigerian civil war broke out. Immediately after the civil war the couple moved back to Lagos with their new born baby (Daniel Iworiso-Markson) where he was raised alongside  his other siblings.

Education

Education Profile of Daniel Iworosi-Markson

He attended St. Andrews Primary School, Apapa, Lagos from 1976 to 1981 and was later transferred to Alaba Primary School, Alaba-Oro in Ajegunle, Lagos, where he obtained his First School Leaving Certificate (FSLC) in 1982.

A strong Foundation

His secondary school education was at Government Secondary School, Bacita, Kwara State where he obtained his West African School Certificate (WASC) in 1987. Before advancing his educational career, he worked for several years with the Federal Radio Corporation of Nigeria (FRCN) on contract as a freelance journalist, presenter/anchorman and script writer.

A Determined Individual

While on the job, he seized the opportunity to further his education where he enrolled, wrote and passed the Business Education Examinations Council (BEEC) professional diploma exams in Advertising in 1997

Destined for great things

This earned him direct entry admission to the University of Nigeria, Nsukka (UNN) where he bagged a Post Graduate Diploma Certificate (PGD) in Marketing (2004) and a Masters of Science Degree (MSc.) in Public Relations from the same University of Nigeria, Nsukka (UNN) in 2006.  He also attended numerous professional courses and international seminars on strategic communications, Public Relations and branding.

Work Experience

His work experience spans over 30 years of consistent communications practice spanning journalism, advertising, public relations, events, sponsorships and corporate social responsibility. He is one of Nigeria’s leading experts in political communication, leadership transition and rebranding. He is reputed for managing the political campaigns of notable political personalities in Nigeria such as the late Engr. Funso Williams who was a top contender in  governorship elections in Lagos State in 1999, 2003 and 2007

Daniel Iworiso-Markson recently served as Honourable Commissioner for Information, Strategy and Orientation, where he won public admiration and almost universal acclaim for the quality of his work and how in eight years he helped put Bayelsa State on the map of national discourse and a worthy example of a state that placed its people at the heart of governance.

As Commissioner for Information & Strategy, he was responsible for a whole range of tasks including articulation and execution of policy, determination of editorial direction, crisis management, media management, communications and team management. He led the team that developed the strategies and executes various activities in the management of media relations, social media communications, internal communications, government stakeholders’ relations and social interactions for positive engagement. He has deep knowledge of the working of the local and international media, government and key agencies and also maintains a wide network of contacts and friends in the media, social media, various state governments and the Federal Government.

Currently, his media consultancy firm, First Media Network Limited, provides strategic communications for the Presidential Amnesty Programme (PAP), which today is witnessing unprecedented transformation under the leadership of the Interim Administrator, Col. Milland Dixon Dikio (Rtd), who since his assumption of office has succeeded in making PAP a most pivotal agency of government that is central to securing and ensuring the sustenance of peace in the Niger Delta.

As a core professional with special bias for public policy and good governance, Daniel Iworiso-Markson has been involved in all forms of public and strategic communications aimed at engaging with a variety of key stakeholders and publics. He has in various capacities been responsible for producing content that tells positive stories, influences narratives and perceptions in ways that have engendered renewed hope for specific target publics, audiences and clients through various promotional enhancements.

  • He is a member of several professional bodies, such as the African Public Relations Association (APRA); Nigerian Institute of Public Relations (NIPR); Public Relations Consultants of Nigeria (PRCAN) and Advertising Practitioners Council of Nigeria (APCON).
  • He is also a member of various social clubs and interest groups, such as Ikoyi Club 1938, HSD Golf & Country Club, South-South/South-East Professional Association and acts as patron of some charity organizations and foundations in Nigeria.
  • Daniel has authored two books, written numerous published articles, drama scripts on radio and television and has also edited four books: A compilation of speeches of His Excellency, Senator Henry Seriake Dickson, the immediate past Governor of Bayelsa State.
  • He owns DMB Public Relations Limited, a consultancy firm that has offices in Lagos and Abuja. He also sits on the board of several companies, including owning and operating an online daily newspaper Firstnewsonline.ng and the wave-making weekly newspaper FIRST NEWS.
  • He is happily married and blessed with three adult children. His hobbies are reading, travelling and golfing.
